what it is

A theory of consciousness, compiled to a runtime.

Global Workspace Theory — Bernard Baars, 1988 — models the mind as a theater: a vast unconscious audience of parallel specialist processes, a small stage of working memory, and a spotlight of attention. Specialists compete for the stage; whatever wins is broadcast to everyone. Consciousness, on this view, is the broadcast state.

LIMEN builds exactly that organization around a frozen LLM. Ten specialists bid every tick. An auction scores the bids. A threshold — the limen — decides what ignites into a workspace of roughly a paragraph. Winners are broadcast; everything else never happened, cognitively. Around the loop: four memory systems, a clock with a future, interoceptive self-measurement, and fork-and-merge deliberation.

how it works · the cognitive cycle

Everything the mind does happens inside one loop.

One tick, nine phases, fixed order. Planning, remembering, speaking, worrying — all of it is content competing for the same small stage.

1

Wake

Advance the clock. Roll the token-budget day.

2

Futures

The timekeeper fires what is due: reminders, recurrences, tripped dead-man switches. Each arrives as a percept from future_self — a message the mind wrote to a later version of itself.

3

Sense

Poll the senses and drain the inbox. User messages, watched files, and feed digests become percepts — channels supply salience hints; the auction decides what matters. Everything fresh is logged.

4

Bid

Every specialist runs concurrently on an identical view of the conscious contents and returns proposals. LLM work happens here — its output must then compete for consciousness like everything else. A crashing specialist costs one tick of participation, not the mind.

5

Auction

Each bid is scored: salience × novelty × ¬habituation × goal-relevance, with coalitions pooling strength. The formula is below — try it.

6

Ignition — or idle

⚡ top bid ≥ 0.25 · ignition

Winners fill the workspace (oversized thoughts are truncated to fit — capacity forces abstraction), get logged as broadcasts, and the act phase runs: specialists respond to the newly conscious content through a sandboxed, fully logged toolbelt.

· top bid < 0.25 · idle

Nothing becomes conscious. The idle streak grows — feeding the confusion index, waking the mind-wandering specialist, and eventually triggering sleep.

7

Decay

Workspace TTLs age out unrefreshed content after 3 ticks. Habituation fades.

8

Sleep?

Every 24 ticks — or after 6 idle ones — the mind consolidates: replay the episodic log, distill lessons, write them as decaying beliefs, prune the forgotten.

9

Express

Queued speech is handed to the interface layer. The mind proposes utterances; the harness delivers them.

how it works · the auction

One formula decides what becomes conscious.

Salience is the specialist's own urgency estimate — trusted, but modulated. Novelty discounts repetition (a mind that can't is a mind that ruminates). Habituation fatigues chronic winners. Goal-relevance is top-down attention. Weak evidence that agrees can outbid strong evidence standing alone.

priority = salience × (0.40 + 0.60·novelty) × (1 − 0.70·habituation) × (0.50 + 0.50·goal) + coalitions // ignites iff ≥ 0.25

how it works · the audience

Ten specialists. One iron rule: they never talk to each other.

The workspace is the only channel. For one processor to influence another, its content must win the auction and become conscious. That bottleneck isn't an implementation shortcut — it is the theory, and it's what makes the whole mind auditable as a single stream.

perception

Classifies raw percepts; extracts “remind me…” into schedulable requests.

goals

Turns conscious questions into goals; nags when they go stale.

planner

Drafts a numbered plan when a question is conscious and unplanned.

critic

Bids the strongest objection against any conscious plan or verdict.

librarian

Involuntary memory: bids relevant beliefs and skills back onto the stage.

introspector

Turns interoceptive alarms — confusion, low budget — into percepts.

oracle

Forks an ensemble on hard questions; reports the verdict with its dissent.

scribe

Writes conscious verdicts and user decisions into the belief ledger.

speaker

Drafts replies — and must hear its own draft become conscious before delivering it.

wanderer

On idle streaks, bids faint random associations. Almost all stay sub-liminal. By design.

how it works · memory

Append, decay, reinterpret — never silently overwrite.

episodic — what happened

An append-only JSONL autobiography: every broadcast, tool call, alarm, contradiction, and sleep. The stream of consciousness is a file you can grep. The mind never rewrites its history — it reinterprets it.

ledger — what is believed

Claims with provenance, tags, status. Nothing is believed without a source. Confidence decays on a half-life; restatement reinforces by noisy-OR; a reversal is reconciled, cross-linked, and kept — the losing belief is marked, never deleted.

c(t) = c · 0.5^(Δt / half_life)   c′ = 1 − (1−c)(1 − 0.6·c_new)

skills — how to do things

Procedures the mind writes for itself as markdown, surfaced back by the librarian when their triggers match the stage. Its competence is partly files it wrote — inspectable, editable, yours.

sleep — the process between them

Replay the log → distill through the model at temperature 0 → write lessons as decaying beliefs → prune what fell below the floor. Replay, abstraction, forgetting — consolidation implemented as literally as possible.

how it works · self-measurement

It measures its own confusion — then acts on the feeling.

interoception

Per-tick vitals: ignition rate, fork disagreement, tool failures, budget remaining. One headline number; threshold crossings re-enter consciousness as alarms, so the mind can hedge, ask, or defer spending because of how it feels.

confusion = 0.4·(1−ignition) + 0.4·disagreement + 0.2·failures

population — fork, diff, merge

On hard questions the oracle forks persona stances — analyst, skeptic, optimist — in parallel, clusters the answers by meaning, and measures disagreement (a zero-dependency cousin of semantic entropy). The merged verdict keeps the dissent; a divided ensemble is stored weakly and spoken with honest hedging.

how to use it

From clone to inner life in four commands.

Python 3.11+ and nothing else. The mock cortex is deterministic, so the demo below reproduces byte-for-byte.

i

Watch a life

A scripted 26-tick day — the trace above — plus the 84-test suite and an offline ablation matrix: what does each mechanism buy?

$ git clone https://github.com/bwcummings1/limen.git && cd limen $ python -m limen demo $ python -m unittest discover -s tests # 84 tests, < 1s $ python -m evals.run # ablations: no habituation ⇒ rumination
ii

Talk to it

Your lines become stimuli. Watch bids with --trace; let it idle with /tick.

$ python -m limen run $ python -m limen ask "Should we rewrite billing in Rust?" --trace $ python -m limen inspect beliefs # …workspace | metrics | episodic
iii

Give it a real cortex

One env var, one config line. Budget caps, retries, caching, and per-purpose model routing are already wired — cheap models for the persona forks, a strong one where memory gets written.

$ export ANTHROPIC_API_KEY=sk-ant-… # limen.toml — the payload auto-adapts per model generation [provider] kind = "anthropic" model = "claude-opus-4-8" [provider.models] # optional routing by purpose oracle = "claude-haiku-4-5"
iv

Let it live

The daemon maps ticks onto wall time; state persists across restarts. Point the senses at the world and the auction triages what arrives.

# limen.toml [sensors] watch_dirs = ["~/notes/inbox"] # file changes become percepts rss_feeds = ["https://…/feed.xml"] $ python -m limen daemon --period 5 # one tick / 5s · Ctrl-C to stop
v

Or embed it — the whole mind is one object

from limen import Mind mind = Mind.from_config("limen.toml") mind.stimulate("Should we migrate the blog?") replies, trace = mind.run_until_response() # a custom specialist is ~20 lines — see docs/SPECIALISTS.md

why

The model already thinks. The loop gives it a life.

A request–response model has no future, no past, no self-measure, and no second opinion. Every one of those is scaffolding — and scaffolding is buildable. This is what the loop buys:

Patience

Intentions persist across ticks and restarts. “Remind me in a bit” becomes a message from your past self that fires on time, unprompted.

Initiative

Dead-man switches trigger on absence — no heartbeat, no check-in — so silence itself can wake the mind.

Memory with provenance

Every belief has sources, a half-life, and an audit trail. Reversals are reconciled in the open; the losing side stays on the record.

Calibrated self-doubt

Fork disagreement is measured, not vibed — it flows into stored confidence and honest hedging. A divided mind says so.

what LIMEN is not

It is not a claim that anything here is conscious. It implements the functional organization GWT describes — parallel specialists, a capacity-limited broadcast bottleneck, ignition, habituation, sleep. Whether that organization suffices for experience is the hard problem, and a few hundred lines of Python does not settle it. Not a brain model either: ticks aren't milliseconds, and seven items is a homage, not a measurement. The intelligence is rented; the architecture is the contribution.

Most of what we call an inner life is an operating system — and operating systems are buildable.
